### BANANAS

**What the invoices say:** Chiquita/Dole 18KG box, consistently £18.50 across all three years. Rock solid price — no seasonal swings, no origin surprises. One of the most dependable costs in your entire shop.

**The maths:** 18KG at £18.50 = £1.03/kg. A typical banana bunch (4–5 bananas, ~500g) costs you around 51p.

**What you should charge:** £1.49 per bunch is fine and delivers 65%+ margin. This is your best-margin everyday staple. Never discount this.

**The trick:** Because the cost never changes, staff can get lazy and stop checking. Watch for creeping box weight — occasionally a box arrives light. Weigh a few randomly.

**Never do this:** Don't buy on impulse at the market if you've already got stock. Bananas ripen whether you sell them or not.

### AVOCADO

**What the invoices say:** Spain 18-count ready-to-eat trays at £16–16.50 most of the time. April 2024 saw a massive 20-box bulk buy at £15.50 (360 avocados in one go). Brazil occasionally at £8/8-count = £1/each but rare. SA Hass 10KG jumble crate at £6.50 appeared in May 2026 — very cheap.

**The maths:** Spain 18-count at £16 = **89p per avocado**. You sell them at £1.09. That's only 20p margin — one of the tightest in your shop.

**What you should charge:** £1.49 minimum. £1.69 is not unreasonable — Ocado charges £1.25 for an inferior product. At £1.49 your margin becomes 40%, which is what it should be.

**The seasonal trick:** March–April is the sweet spot for Spanish avocados. Price and quality are best. By summer, origin shifts and costs can spike. The SA Hass jumble crate in May 2026 at £6.50/10KG (65p each) is a brilliant buy if the quality is there — snap those up when they appear.

**Watch out for:** "Ready to eat" avocados from Dole move fast but you're paying a premium for the ripening done by someone else. If you're selling next-day, that premium is worth it. If you're stocking for 3 days, buy firmer and ripen yourself.

### CITRUS — ORANGES

**What the invoices say:** Two very different price tiers exist and you're buying both.

- **Spain Navel (Podium grade) 45/15KG:** £20–22 (January peak). This is the premium product.
- **Spain Navel (Citrus King) 45/15KG:** £14 — same size orange, different brand, same delivery day. **That's a £6 difference for the identical product.** Always ask which brand.
- **Egypt Valencia 80/15KG:** £7–12. Collapses to £7 at end of season (April). Huge volumes but lower quality.
- **Turkey Navel 80/15KG:** £8–8.50. Budget option.
- **SA Navel 40/15KG:** £20. New from 2026 — replaces Spain late season.
- **Morocco Navel 70/15KG:** £14. Appearing May 2026 — good late-season value.

**The maths:** Spain Podium at £20/45 units = 44p each. Selling at 69p large = 36% margin. That's ok but not great.

**What you should charge:** 29p small, 69p large is what's on EPOS. The large is borderline — consider 79p. The small at 29p is fine if it's the Egypt/Turkey origin (cost 10–17p each).

**The golden rule:** Never sell Egyptian Valencia oranges as a premium product. They're a volume play — cheap and cheerful. The Spain Navel (Podium) is your quality product. Price them differently on the shop floor.

**When to stock up:** January–February for Spanish Navels (best quality and most choice). April onward switches to South African origin — same price, different taste. Egypt Valencias are end-of-April clearance — buy them cheap and sell them cheap.

### CITRUS — LEMONS

**What the invoices say:** Spain 30/6KG boxes ranging from £6 (cheap, early 2024) to £9.50 (March 2026). Huge volatility. Also Spain 60/15KG bulk at £22 (March 2026) — much bigger format. SA lemon now appearing at £25/15KG.

**The maths:** Spain 30/6KG at £7.50 = 25p each. SA at £25/15KG with ~113 lemons = 22p each but you're paying more per box.

**What you should charge:** 40p each on the EPOS. That's 37% margin at typical price. Fine but could be 49p for 49% margin without anyone noticing.

**The trick:** Spanish lemon prices move fast. In summer they can drop to £5.50/30-count. Winter they stay high at £8–9.50. Never batch-buy lemons — they don't keep and the price will come down.

**Watch out for SA lemons:** £25/15KG at 22p cost is fine on margin but 113-count = smaller lemons. Customers notice size. Only buy if you're selling by weight.

### STRAWBERRIES

**What the invoices say:** Three completely different price tiers.

- **Spain 16×250G (Frez Luz, Driscoll, Cuna):** £12.80–20.80/box = 80p–£1.30 per 250G punnet. This is your everyday strawberry.
- **Spain 1KG loose:** £3.50–7.20/KG depending on season. Cheapest option.
- **Belgium 8×500G (Hoogstraten, Driscoll Gold, REO):** £18–32/box = £2.25–4 per 500G punnet. Premium product. **June 2026 update: Hoogstraten dropped to £18/8-pack = £2.25/punnet — the cheapest Belgium strawberry in three years of data.**
- **UK 20×400G (Nationwide):** £54/box = £2.70 per 400G punnet. Peak UK season only (May–June).

**The maths on Belgium:** You sell Belgium at £3.60/500G punnet. At the old cost of £3.44–4, that was break-even or a loss. At today's £18/8-pack = £2.25/punnet, you now make £1.35 gross profit at £3.60 (37%) — but you should still be charging **£4.99**. The cost drop is your profit, not your customer's discount. At £4.99 and £2.25 cost = **55% margin**.

**What you should charge:**
- Belgium Hoogstraten punnet: **£4.99–5.49.** Ocado charges £4 for a worse product. You're selling the real thing below cost.
- Spain 250G punnet at 80p cost: Sell at £1.80 = 56% margin ✅ (you already do this — keep it)
- UK strawberry season (May–June): These cost £2.70/400G but they're special. Mark up to £3.99–4.50 for that "local/British" premium.

**The seasonal playbook:**
- **March–April:** Spain early season. Quality variable but cheap (80p/punnet). Stock heavily.
- **May–June:** Belgium peaks. Never discount. Spain Spain is everywhere — use Spain for everyday, Belgium for premium/weekend.
- **July onward:** Spanish dominates. UK for the premium slot. No Belgian at that point.
- **November–February:** Strawberry season is essentially over. Only Belgium survives at premium price. Don't chase them.

### STONE FRUIT — PEACHES & NECTARINES

**What the invoices say:** Spain 10×500G (5KG prepacked) season runs April–June. First appearance: late April (25 Apr 2024, 25 Apr 2025). Peak volume: May–June.

- Nectarine Spain 10×500G prepacked: **£14–18/box** = £1.40–1.80 per 500G punnet (May pricing)
- Nectarine Spain 10×1KG (Lison, 10KG): **£15/box** = £1.50/kg = **75p per 500G** — new June 2026 format, 25% cheaper per kg than the 5KG format
- Peach Spain 10×500G: **£15–17/box** = £1.50–1.70 per 500G punnet
- Peach Spain 10×1KG (Lison, 10KG): **£15/box** = £1.50/kg — same large format, confirmed June 2026
- Peach Donut Spain 10×500G prepacked (Distinet): **£10–18/box** = £1.00–1.80 per 500G punnet. **June 2026: dropped to £10** = biggest price drop of the year
- Peach Donut Spain 5KG loose (AA Poupart): £14/box = £2.80/kg — more expensive format, less good value

**What you should charge:**
- Nectarine Punnet 500G: **£2.50** (at £0.75/500G June cost = 70% margin ✅)
- Peach Punnet 500G: £2.50 ✅
- Donut Peach: **£2.99** (at £1.00/500G June cost = 67% margin ✅)
- Loose nectarine/peach per piece: **75p minimum** (currently 45p = running at loss)

**Always buy prepacked Distinet format for Donut Peach** — it costs less per kg than the loose box and is ready to display. If only loose is available, £14 is acceptable; above £14 walk away.

**Critical error — Nectarine Punnet 1kg LARGE at £2.99:** At old May costs (£1.55/500G), two punnets = £3.10 cost vs £2.99 sell = loss. At June cost (£0.75/500G), two punnets = £1.50 cost vs £2.99 sell = 50% margin. The 1KG entry is fine at June prices — but check monthly as costs change.

**The seasonal trick:** Holland also sells nectarines (X24–X30 per box, 28-count at £4–6/box in June–July = 14–21p each!). These are loose single nectarines, not punnets. At 14p cost and 75p retail price = 81% margin. If David can source these loose boxes in July, that's the best stone fruit margin of the year.

**When to push:** May and June are your strawberry and stone fruit moment. These are the months when customers are in the mood, the product is perfect, and the margins on stone fruit can be exceptional. This is when to have peaches prominently displayed, have samples, be loud about it.
